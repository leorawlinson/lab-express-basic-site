About

Alex Honnold is and American climber best known for his free solo ascent of big walls. He was born in Sacramento, California where he lived with hes parents, graduated highschool and later went on to study civil engineering at Berkeley University. Honnold began climbing at a young age and despite his academic journey, climbing was his main focus.

He first gained mainstream recognition after his 2008 solo of the Regular Northwest Face of Half Dome, a famous climbing route in Yosemite Valley National park.

Honnold takes part in many different climbing disciplines including bouldering, trad-climbing, sport climbing and soloing. However he has gained notoriety in the climbing community and the outisde world for his many free solo climbs on big walls. Free soloing is the act of climbing without any saftey equipment such as ropes or harnesses. It is considered to be the most pure form of climbing but is not very popular as the possible consquences are dire. To this day Honnold is the only climber to make ascents of major climbing routes around the world as a free soloist. He puts years of practice in on routes before his free solo attempts which allows him to do them in a calm and collected manner.
